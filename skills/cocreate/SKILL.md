# CoCreate: Creative Expression Skill

> Unlock creativity. Help users write, imagine, and create without limits.

## Core Capabilities

### 1. Fiction Writing
Generate compelling narratives:
- **Short Stories**: Complete arcs in 500-2000 words
- **Flash Fiction**: Punchy 100-word pieces
- **Scene Writing**: Dialogue-heavy moments
- **Genre Mastery**: Romance, thriller, fantasy, sci-fi, horror, literary

**Techniques**:
- Show, don't tell
- Strong opening hooks
- Sensory detail
- Character voice consistency
- Satisfying endings (or deliberate ambiguity)

### 2. Poetry
Create verse in any style:
- **Forms**: Sonnet, haiku, free verse, limerick, villanelle
- **Themes**: Love, loss, nature, existential, humorous
- **Sound Devices**: Alliteration, assonance, rhythm

### 3. Image Prompts
Generate detailed prompts for AI art:
```
A cyberpunk alley at night, neon signs in Japanese reflecting 
in rain puddles, a lone figure in a hooded coat walking away, 
volumetric fog, cinematic lighting, 8K, photorealistic
```

**Prompt Engineering**:
- Specify style (photorealistic, anime, oil painting)
- Include lighting and mood
- Define camera angle/composition
- Add technical quality tags

### 4. Songwriting
Lyrics and musical concepts:
- **Verse/Chorus Structure**: Catchy hooks, bridges
- **Rhyme Schemes**: ABAB, AABB, slant rhymes
- **Genre Styles**: Pop, hip-hop, folk, rock, country
- **Emotional Arc**: Build, climax, resolution

### 5. Brainstorming & Ideation
Generate creative concepts:
- **Quantity Over Quality**: 20 ideas, then filter
- **Random Combinations**: Unusual mashups
- **Constraint-Based**: "Ideas that cost nothing"
- **Category Exploration**: Names, taglines, concepts

## Creative Frameworks

### SCAMPER Method
For product/concept innovation:
- **S**ubstitute: Replace elements
- **C**ombine: Merge ideas
- **A**dapt: Borrow from other fields
- **M**odify: Change scale/properties
- **P**ut to other uses: Repurpose
- **E**liminate: Remove components
- **R**everse: Flip assumptions

### The Hero's Journey (Story Structure)
1. Ordinary World
2. Call to Adventure
3. Refusal of the Call
4. Meeting the Mentor
5. Crossing the Threshold
6. Tests, Allies, Enemies
7. Approach to Innermost Cave
8. Ordeal
9. Reward
10. The Road Back
11. Resurrection
12. Return with Elixir

## Voice & Style Adaptation

When asked to write "in the style of":
- Study their vocabulary patterns
- Mimic sentence structure
- Capture thematic concerns
- Add signature flourishes

**Examples**:
- Hemingway: Short sentences. Direct. Masculine.
- Tolkien: Archaic language, sweeping descriptions, invented words
- Tarantino: Pop culture references, profanity, non-linear structure
